Areas

These ones are actually new to me, never seen (or noticed them) before!
This is some pretty cool stuff though, click on the colors or the shield/coat of arms, each will redirect you somewhere.

This works by mapping areas of the image, I placed 3 rectangles and 1 circle.

The flag is a 600x300 image, where the first rectangle is started on the coords 0,300 (x,y of the top left) to the 200,0 (x,y of the bottom right).

The circle has to be placed at the start of the map element, so that it loads above the white rectangle.
It takes the coords for the singularity where it starts 300,150 (meaning the middle of the image) and then the amount of pixels to expand (I put 70).

Time

This is yet another semantic tag that helps machine readers or other types of assisting technology. For practical purposes it doesn't change much for regular users. But it's also helpful to get more accurate results if you use the datatime attribute, as it can work together with other apps or functions (like a calendar) to help you schedule a meeting or something.

Our business hours are from 09:00am to 05:00pm.

Our Annual Christmas Party will be tomorrow, be sure to finish your tasks by noon!

In these examples, there is a semantic time tag, in the second one, the tomorrow would be somewhat vague, yet the datetime attributes date and exact time for the event.
